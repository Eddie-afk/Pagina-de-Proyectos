Game information

History

In Five Nights at Freddy's 2, the player takes on the role of Jeremy Fitzgerald, a security guard working at Freddy Fazbear's Pizza. For five nights, Jeremy must keep an eye on the animatronics, which become dangerous at night. The pizzeria has introduced new models of animatronics, but also keeps the old, worn-out ones. Throughout the nights, clues about dark events are revealed, such as the mysterious "Bite of '87" and disappearances linked to the animatronics. Jeremy must survive as the animatronics try to catch him.

Development

Five Nights at Freddy's 2 was developed quickly by Scott Cawthon following the success of the first game in 2014. This sequel introduced more animatronics, both old and new, and changed the gameplay mechanics: instead of doors, the player wears a mask to avoid attacks. The game, created with Clickteam Fusion 2.5, expanded on the franchise's lore, introducing key characters such as The Puppet and referencing the mysterious "Bite of '87". FNAF 2 was a hit that cemented the series' popularity.

Mechanics

You must keep an eye on cameras, wear a Freddy mask to fool the animatronics, and use the flashlight to keep Foxy at bay. Additionally, you must keep a music box activated to prevent The Puppet from attacking you. The animatronics also move through vents, so you need to keep an eye on everything at once to survive.

Who developed FNAF?

Scott Cawthon

Scott Cawthon is an independent video game developer best known for creating Five Nights at Freddy's. Prior to FNAF, he worked on Christian games that were unsuccessful. Inspired by criticism of his previous work, he created FNAF in 2014 using the Clickteam Fusion 2.5 engine. The game was a huge success, and Scott went on to develop several sequels and expand the franchise. He is known for his solitary approach to development, controlling the programming, art, and narrative of his games.
